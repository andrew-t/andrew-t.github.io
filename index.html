Maths

Somehow, over the years, I have become involved in a surprising number of silly mathematical projects.

Domino Computer

In 2012, I helped Matt Parker design and build a computer entirely out of dominoes.

MegaMenger

In 2014, Matt Parker and Laura Taalman ran the the MegaMenger project, with the aim of building a giant Menger sponge from card.

I built some of the Manchester build, as well as the image gallery for the project, which may be the world’s first 2.7D website.

The Thingometer

I made this during a leaders’ debate in the run up to the 2010 UK election, to allow people to create a hybrid party leader who could truly represent their views, or to visualise polling data in the worst imaginable way.

Obviously nearly all of these people have resigned at least once since then.

Twitterbots

A look at the silly Twitter accounts I have programmed my Pi to run, and the logic behind them (such as it is).

Games

A selection of daft browser games I have made, including a 3D sudoku, a block-matching game for a wedding, and a wholly-original AR monster-catching game (which sadly only works in Chrome for Android).

Megapixel

In 2016, I helped Katie Steckles and Matt Parker build the Megapixel, a digital display made out of transparent plastic and red, green and blue OHP markers.
